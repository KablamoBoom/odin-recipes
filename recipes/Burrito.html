Burrito

A burrito is a dish in Mexican and Tex-Mex cuisine that took form in California cuisine, consisting of a flour tortilla wrapped into a sealed cylindrical shape around various ingredients. The tortilla is sometimes lightly grilled or steamed to soften it, make it more pliable, and allow it to adhere to itself. Burritos are often eaten by hand, as their tight wrapping keeps the ingredients together. Burritos are filled with savory ingredients, most often a meat such as beef, chicken, or pork, and often include other ingredients, such as rice, cooked beans (either whole or refried), vegetables, such as lettuce and tomatoes, cheese, and condiments such as salsa, pico de gallo, guacamole, or crema.

Ingredients

  • 1 flour tortilla
  • 1/2cup shredded cheese
  • 1/2cup boiled rice
  • 1/2cup cut, grilled chicken breast
  • 1/2can refried beans
  • 2tbsp salsa
  • 3tbsp sour cream
  • 1/4cup diced tomatoes

Steps

  1. Spread cheese across tortilla, 2inches away from rim
  2. Put tortilla and cheese in microwave for 45 seconds
  3. Spread rice across cheese, stir in salsa
  4. Create a line of beans across middle of rice
  5. Add chicken pieces parallel to beans
  6. Add sour cream parallel to beans
  7. Add tomatoes parallel to beans
  8. Fold in sides of burrito perpendicular to beans, covering all ingredients on each side
  9. Roll burrito parallel to ingredients
  10. Microwave for 1 minute
